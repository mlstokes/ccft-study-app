# CCFT Content Outline (V3.0)

## D1 — Screening and Ongoing Assessment (8%, 11 items)

### 1.1 Determine the athlete's readiness for training
- 1.1.1 Screen athlete for medical issues (e.g., health questionnaire)
- 1.1.2 Recognize conditions and injuries outside of Certified CrossFit Trainer Scope of Practice that require referral (i.e., risk factors)
- 1.1.3 Assess for rhabdomyolysis ("rhabdo") risk
- 1.1.4 Assess psychological tolerance for training

### 1.2 Assess and monitor the athlete's fitness goals and performance
- 1.2.1 Assess capacity and capability to perform functional movements
- 1.2.2 Evaluate athlete's work capacity
- 1.2.3 Identify athlete's fitness goals
- 1.2.4 Monitor athlete for signs of over- or under-training
- 1.2.5 Identify when athlete is ready to progress to another goal/skill
- 1.2.6 Quantify and track performance

## D2 — Programming (14%, 19 items)

### 2.1 Design workouts at an individual and group level
- 2.1.1 Design single workouts using the CrossFit methodology
- 2.1.2 Design a sequence of workouts that will improve general physical preparedness (GPP)

### 2.2 Create scaling options to optimize results for categories of athletes
- 2.2.1 Scale workouts to optimize efficacy by age group
- 2.2.2 Scale workouts to optimize efficacy for special populations (e.g., pregnant people, adaptive)
- 2.2.3 Scale workouts to optimize efficacy for athletes with sport-specific goals
- 2.2.4 Design programming for injured athletes to maintain fitness and restore functionality
- 2.2.5 Scale workouts to optimize efficacy by skill level

### 2.3 Evaluate results of programming
- 2.3.1 Evaluate program success relative to athlete goals and needs
- 2.3.2 Use benchmarks to assess effectiveness of programming
- 2.3.3 Adjust programming based on performance and goals
- 2.3.4 Evaluate athlete progress relative to CrossFit's definition of fitness and their personal goal

## D3 — Educating (19%, 27 items)

### 3.1 Teach movements and concepts
- 3.1.1 Teach proper execution of movements
- 3.1.2 Layer instruction to meet athlete capability
- 3.1.3 Use progressions to teach complex moves as necessary
- 3.1.4 Teach and use correct spotting techniques
- 3.1.5 Teach the definition of CrossFit
- 3.1.6 Teach descriptive and defining characteristics of functional movements
- 3.1.7 Teach importance of movements that do not produce much, if any, power (e.g., L-sit, back lever)
- 3.1.8 Teach the CrossFit definition of fitness and health and the four models that illustrate that definition
- 3.1.9 Teach the goals of CrossFit programming and how goals are met (e.g., programming with variance)
- 3.1.10 Teach the relationship between technique and intensity (i.e., threshold training)
- 3.1.11 Teach relative intensity
- 3.1.12 Teach the principles of scaling

### 3.2 Demonstrate movements and concepts
- 3.2.1 Provide demonstration of proper movements
- 3.2.2 Provide demonstration of movement faults
- 3.2.3 Provide demonstration of movement progressions

## D4 — Training (24%, 34 items)

### 4.1 Evaluate athlete movements
- 4.1.1 Identify points of performance
- 4.1.2 Identify sound mechanics
- 4.1.3 Identify faults in movement
- 4.1.4 Identify refinements of sound mechanics to optimize performance
- 4.1.5 Identify common themes in athletes' movements
- 4.1.6 Identify root cause of faults

### 4.2 Facilitate correct movement
- 4.2.1 Communicate cues to correct unsound mechanics
- 4.2.2 Use multiple cueing strategies (visual, tactile, and verbal)
- 4.2.3 Utilize effective cues (short, specific, actionable)
- 4.2.4 Reinforce sound mechanics
- 4.2.5 Refine sound mechanics to optimize performance
- 4.2.6 Apply basic principles of anatomy and biomechanics
- 4.2.7 Address the most egregious fault first (triage)
- 4.2.8 Balance relentlessness with acknowledgement of improvement

## D5 — Leadership and Management (16%, 23 items)

### 5.1 Inspire, motivate, and engage athletes
- 5.1.1 Provide athletes with goal setting
- 5.1.2 Create a culture and community of excellence and camaraderie
- 5.1.3 Establish rapport with athletes and adapt approach based on athlete response
- 5.1.4 Lead by example

### 5.2 Manage athletes and groups
- 5.2.1 Apply the strategy of mechanics, consistency, and intensity to CrossFit programming to optimize athlete safety and performance
- 5.2.2 Apply scaling options to meet the current physical and psychological needs of each athlete
- 5.2.3 Manage risk for the athlete
- 5.2.4 Plan lessons for a class period (including general warm-up, skill development session, workout, and cool-down)
- 5.2.5 Engage virtual athletes
- 5.2.6 Recognize warning signs for possible injury or overexertion
- 5.2.7 Arrange equipment, athletes, and instructor(s) to maximize safety
- 5.2.8 Educate athletes on personal responsibility in a workout setting
- 5.2.9 Balance attention across individuals while maintaining group cohesion
- 5.2.10 Manage logistics (including instructor-to-athlete ratio, equipment demands, workout duration, and effective utilization of space)
- 5.2.11 Organize athletes, space, equipment, and time to achieve workout objectives

## D6 — Lifestyle Education (11%, 15 items)

### 6.1 Provide nutrition education
- 6.1.1 Identify nutrition goals
- 6.1.2 Assess athlete's nutrition
- 6.1.3 Provide nutrition guidance
- 6.1.4 Review basic health markers (e.g., HDL, triglycerides, hemoglobin A1C, body fat percentage, lean-body mass, blood pressure, bone density)
- 6.1.5 Recognize nutrition scope of practice limitations
- 6.1.6 Outline dietary strategies (including food types and amounts)
- 6.1.7 Teach athletes how to read food labels
- 6.1.8 Teach athletes how to build sample meal plans

### 6.2 Provide additional lifestyle education
- 6.2.1 Identify additional lifestyle goals (e.g., alcohol or substance use, sleep/recovery, stress)
- 6.2.2 Assess athlete's lifestyle
- 6.2.3 Provide additional lifestyle guidance
- 6.2.4 Recognize scope-of-practice limitations

## D7 — Professional Responsibilities (8%, 11 items)

### 7.1 Engage in professional development
- 7.1.1 Self-assess individual and group training sessions according to the foundations of effective training
- 7.1.2 Pursue continuing education

### 7.2 Manage risk for the business entity and the facility
- 7.2.1 Obtain informed consent and waiver of liability from every athlete consistent with local regulations
- 7.2.2 Obtain written and signed medical release from athlete, when required
- 7.2.3 Maintain and inspect equipment and keep facility clean and safe
- 7.2.4 Recognize and respond to extreme environmental conditions
- 7.2.5 Assess insurance policy and other legal needs (e.g., liability, employment status, truth in advertising)

### 7.3 Prepare for a medical emergency and equipment or facility malfunction
- 7.3.1 Prepare and practice response to facility emergencies, medical emergencies, and injuries (i.e., create and follow an emergency action plan or EAP)

### 7.4 Run an ethical practice
- 7.4.1 Work within Certified CrossFit Trainer Scope of Practice
- 7.4.2 Adhere to CrossFit Standards of Professional Practice and other related legal agreements
